Sermon Series: NOPE

Saying NOPE to Pride

An interactive study app inspired by Jake Musselman's message on moving away from the "you first" lens of pride and embracing the countercultural path of humility.

The Sandbox of Life

The Kindergarten Pickup Line

"We’re all clearly there to pick up children because no sane person would be waiting in this line..." Pride subtly manifests when we act as if our immediate schedule, comfort, and preferences are the center of the universe—even in the daily mundane spaces like a school parking loop.

Core Concepts

Click panels to expand details

"Pride sees culture, others, and God as tools for our self-fulfillment."

  • We often protect our deep fear of humiliation by arming ourselves with pride (status, material wealth, loudness).
  • When we use people as tools, we fail to feel bad because "tools are made to be used" — a destructive illusion that started with the first lie in the garden.
  • James connects spiritual pride directly with unfaithfulness ("You adulterous people").
1. Worth

Humility is not self-deprecation. True humility requires recognizing your God-given worth, gifts, and status, then willingly choosing to direct them outward.

2. Willing

Humiliation is forced upon you, but humility is a daily choice. It is the active submission to God’s rhythm over our immediate convenience.

3. World

Not an abstract space, but "your" immediate world. It is redirecting your real power, resources, and attention for the tangible benefit of the people in your life.

"It's not really a small thing when in small things we resist self." — Thomas à Kempis

Saying "Nope" to pride starts in the unsung, quiet moments: holding your tongue when someone cuts you off in conversation, giving up a premium parking space, listening before offering a louder counterargument, and using your unique professional influence to champion co-workers rather than out-climb them.
