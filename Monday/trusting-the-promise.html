Lesson Overview
Genesis Series

1. Testing vs. Tempting

God tests to reveal and refine genuine faith (v.1); Satan tempts to destroy. Testing shows us where our trust actually rests under pressure.

2. Faith Expressed in Prompt Action

Abraham rose "early the next morning" (v.3). True faith isn't an abstract feeling; it's immediate obedience before seeing the full outcome.

3. Expectant Hope (Yahweh-Jireh)

"God Himself will provide the lamb" (v.8). Abraham trusted God's character and promises even when the logistics seemed impossible (Hebrews 11:17-19).

4. The Ultimate Substitute

Isaac carrying the wood up Mount Moriah foreshadows Christ carrying His cross up Calvary. God spared Abraham's son, but did not spare His own.

Leader Tip for Group Flow If discussion gets bogged down in debate over Abraham's morality or hard questions, pull the group back to Question 10 & 13: Focus on the object of our faith (Jesus) rather than human perfection.
Genesis 22:1-14 (NIV)
Read Aloud Together

1 Some time later God tested Abraham. He said to him, “Abraham!” “Here I am,” he replied.

2 Then God said, “Take your son, your only son, whom you love—Isaac—and go to the region of Moriah. Sacrifice him there as a burnt offering on a mountain I will show you.”

3 Early the next morning Abraham got up and loaded his donkey. He took with him two of his servants and his son Isaac. When he had cut enough wood for the burnt offering, he set out for the place God had told him about.

4 On the third day Abraham looked up and saw the place in the distance.

5 He said to his servants, “Stay here with the donkey while I and the boy go over there. We will worship and then we will come back to you.”

6 Abraham took the wood for the burnt offering and placed it on his son Isaac, and he himself carried the fire and the knife. As the two of them went on together,

7 Isaac spoke up and said to his father Abraham, “Father?” “Yes, my son?” Abraham replied. “The fire and wood are here,” Isaac said, “but where is the lamb for the burnt offering?”

8 Abraham answered, “God himself will provide the lamb for the burnt offering, my son.” And the two of them went on together.

9 When they reached the place God had told him about, Abraham built an altar there and arranged the wood on it. He bound his son Isaac and laid him on the altar, on top of the wood.

10 Then he reached out his hand and took the knife to slay his son.

11 But the angel of the Lord called out to him from heaven, “Abraham! Abraham!” “Here I am,” he replied.

12 “Do not lay a hand on the boy,” he said. “Do not do anything to him. Now I know that you fear God, because you have not withheld from me your son, your only son.”

13 Abraham looked up and there in a thicket he saw a ram caught by its horns. He went over and took the ram and sacrificed it as a burnt offering instead of his son.

14 So Abraham called that place The Lord Will Provide. And to this day it is said, “On the mountain of the Lord it will be provided.”

Cross-Reference Note Check out Hebrews 11:17-19 and James 2:21-23 during discussion if guys ask how Abraham could logically go through with this. Abraham believed God could raise the dead.

10 Mins
ICEBREAKER Casual
What's something you're irrationally afraid of (heights, spiders, etc.)? Tell us about a time it caught you off guard.
INTRODUCTION Sermon Recall
What stood out to you most from this week’s message?
2. Observation (Genesis 22:1-14)
20 Mins
QUESTION 1 Observation
What details in the story reveal Abraham’s faith before God provided the ram?
Key Details: Early rising (v.3), statement "we will come back" (v.5), "God himself will provide" (v.8).
QUESTION 2 Observation
The sermon described three characteristics of faith. What were they and which verses illustrate each one?
QUESTION 3 Observation
What repeated statements about God or Abraham caught your attention?
Notice: "Here I am" (v.1, 11), "Your son, your only son" (v.2, 12), "Went on together" (v.6, 8).
QUESTION 4 Typology
What similarities do you see between Isaac and Christ?
Connections: Beloved only son; carrying wood/cross up the hill; submission to the father; miraculous birth.
3. Interpretation
25 Mins
QUESTION 5 Heart Check
God called Isaac "your son, whom you love." What are the good things in your life that could quietly become more important to you than God?
QUESTION 6 Interpretation
Why do you think God tested Abraham after waiting 25 years for Isaac?
QUESTION 7 Interpretation
What was God revealing about Abraham and about himself?
QUESTION 8 Interpretation
Faith was described as something revealed through action, not just an internal feeling. What is usually harder – the first step or continuing to obey while waiting?
QUESTION 9 Core Doctrine
What is the difference between having faith in your own faith versus having faith in God's character?
QUESTION 10 Key Quote
Pastor Seth said, “We’re not saved by the strength of our faith, but by the object of our faith.” Why is that distinction so important?
QUESTION 11 Interpretation
Abraham repeatedly said, "God will provide," before he knew how everything would work out. What does that teach us about biblical hope? How is expectant faith different from simply wishing something will happen?
QUESTION 12 Practical Response
If someone told you, "I just need to try harder to have faith," how would you respond to them after this week's message?
QUESTION 13 Key Quote
Pastor Seth said, “Faith doesn’t grow by trying harder to trust. Faith grows by seeing more clearly that God is trustworthy.” What does this statement mean to you?
4. Personal Application
20 Mins
QUESTION 14 Action Step
Since faith is something revealed through action, what is a step of obedience you’ve been aware of but keep putting off?
QUESTION 15 Action Step
Abraham said "God will provide" before he saw any evidence of it. Where are you waiting right now for God to provide, and what does it look like to keep trusting while you wait?
QUESTION 16 Gospel Focus
Genesis 22 points forward to Jesus, the Son who was not spared so we could be. How does the cross change the way you face uncertainty in your own life?
QUESTION 17 Closing Commitment
Which of these best describes where you are today:
• I need to love God more than His gifts
• I need to take a step of obedience
• I need to keep trusting while I wait
• I need to remember God’s faithfulness instead of focusing on my fears
